About Us

Nigeria is composed of multiple ethnic groups and cultures and the term Nigerian refers to a citizenship-based civic nationality. Nigerians derive from over 250 ethnic groups and languages. Though there are multiple ethnic groups in Nigeria, economic factors result in significant mobility of Nigerians of multiple ethnic and religious backgrounds to reside in territories in Nigeria that are outside their ethnic or religious background, resulting in the intermixing of the various ethnic and religious groups, especially in Nigeria's cities. The English language is the lingua franca of Nigerians. About 50 percent of Nigerians are Muslims and the other 50 percent are Christians. More

Great Waterways

Nigeria has 3000km of navigable waterways out of 10,000km according to National Inland Waterways Authority (NIWA). Additionally, her coastal location in the gulf of Guinea contributes generously towards economic, maritime, tourism and and sociological growth.

Some Attractions

New African Shrine

The New Afrika Shrine is an open-air entertainment centre located in Ikeja, Lagos State. It serves as the host location of the annual Felabration music festival.

National Theatre

An architectural masterpiece and cultural landmark, the edifice is one of the few properties of the country that has stood the test of time.

Lekki Conservation Centre

Built in 1990, Lekki conservation centre covers a land area of 78 hectares. Come and appreciate plants and animals in their natural habitat.
